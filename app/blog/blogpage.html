Blog/ The Industry

Date: 2nd Oct 16

The Industry

Currently there is an exceptional amount of growth within the technology industry, therefore the need to fill the roles within it increase, but finding the right person for the role can be challenging. The digital tech industry grew 32% faster than the rest of the economy 2010-2014(Tech Nation Report, 2016) .

I have looked at two main articles from the Manchester Digital ‘Skills Summit’ PDF slide deck and the Tech Nation 2016 PDF. Both of these have given me a great insight into how businesses see it from the inside, it allows me to see what they are seeking as well as common issues faced when trying to recruit. Development is a significant function within digital businesses yet is one of the most difficult roles to fill and some businesses are contracting workers in to fill the roles (Skills Summit, 2016) .

From looking at the Skills Summit I can see that MMU is the second most popular university within the northwest for businesses to recruit talent from. This for me is really exciting news. (Skills Summit) “80% of the businesses recruit graduates and 69% have a graduate scheme for students to go into,” to me this shows that businesses do want new talent and also people willing to learn and develop. That is another massive point from within the tech industry, no one ever knows everything. With the industry continuously developing and changing it’s no wonder that one of the the biggest benefit offered to staff (to attract and retain) is career development and training.

The:Ratio

The male:female ratio has been talked about in the tech industry. From the data collected in the Skills Summit, “Technical roles are split 70:30 male to female”, this could be one of the reasons for the shortage in the more technical roles but I do think the ratio will even up more, developers don’t tend to just develop anymore and with people being more aware that it isn’t just anti-social coders will bring more females into starting development roles. The Tech Nation PDF shows that digital tech businesses source talent mainly from “experienced talent from a local cluster” and “universities in a local cluster is second with 35.8%.”

Who knows it all?

Another main point coming from my position is that you cannot be taught everything in university, they give you doors to open and windows to look through but it’s up to you to go through the door! Luckily for me this comes in terms of regular industry speakers and tickets to events such as DPM:UK.These conferences give me a real opening into the world after university. There is also an importance for skills development to digital tech businesses, (Tech Nation) 66.5% self-taught programming and second is in-house training with local universities at 26.8%. I think this shows that if someone has taken the time to teach themselves then it has taken effort, commitment and passion. Employers want to employ someone who wants to expand on their own knowledge and at the same time help the business.

What makes a developer?

The problems I found with these documents are that I feel they could go more into detail about why developers are hard to find and more detail into what the businesses look for in a developer. Especially as it’s the hardest role to fill from universities and colleges, is it the courses, experience or just the lack of self-learning? They could also go into more detail on what each business thinks makes a “developer” and what else they look for apart from the ability to code. Please feel free to let me know what you think.

There is a fantastic article from A List Apart that explains about how people skills are needed for web workers;

There’s a myth that you either have these skills or you don’t—which Meri Williams calls “the soft skills fairy.” But that’s like saying, “You can either code JavaScript or you can’t.” You didn’t fall out of bed with technical skills, and the same is true of people skills.

I think this is very true, with the tech industry growing no one just does a single job anymore, so therefore with me leaving university it means that having a good range of skills and still being able to communicate my point can help me stand up against people that can code amazingly well yet may lack soft skills. Smaller businesses may require more flexible people that can fit into many roles and are happy to adapt to what is asked, especially as some businesses only employ 5 people.

Finally

Finally, I think the future is exciting, the tech industry is really developing and the need for people to work within it will continue to grow, I also think there is a place for most people in the industry, the list of roles continues to grow and I feel that allows more people to see themselves within a role.
