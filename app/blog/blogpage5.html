Website Alterations

Original Site : www.13161412.webdevmmu.uk/portfolio

Building this site has been a big step for me, having to learn so much in such a short space of time – in particular, mobile first and responsive design. Some things need improving, tweaking and altering and some of my code could be cleaned up. My attention had been drawn to the pros and cons of handwritten fonts let me know your thoughts.

Homepage

On the homepage, I altered the flexbox to include flex grow and flex basis which allowed the 3 links to be spaced equally. I also made the whole area clickable rather than just the text to create a larger target area.

Blog

On the blog page I removed the repetitive ‘read more’ link text as well as re-ordering the posts so that the more recent appears first. I also made a hover on desktop so that it had a bottom border, this wasn’t included on mobile as hover acts like a click and therefore it would require two clicks to load the page.

Footer

The next alteration I needed to make was the footer, previously it was none existent and I neglected the vital space that was available. I decided I would use it to include a list of links to other pages around my site as this saves people scrolling back up to the top of my page to move. I also decided to include social links, my GitHub, Twitter and LinkedIn are all there and then my email is available in the centre. I felt these were useful things for people that may view my site and by putting them in the footer it allows them to be available on every page.

Mobile Navigation

The JavaScript used previously for the hamburger menu was getting outdated and I didn’t realise it was considered bad practice until I received feedback. I then decided to research why and ask fellow students and I know realise it is harder to maintain and jQuery is easier to deal with. I have therefore altered the JavaScript to .click jQuery. The jQuery also doesn't requite the onclick="myFunction()" within the html.

I have altered the styling on the mobile navigation links to try and make the navigation look less basic, I took the box around the links away.

General Changes

I have also made general changes to the header on desktop which made it sit in line with my logo rather than below on larger screen sizes. I also have added some links to my blogs so that they can be tweeted out, this makes it far easier for people to share my blogs. I have added a picture of myself on the about page so that people know what I look like and it adds a more personal touch to the site. Previously I didn’t have a H1 on the homepage so I have added one and have hidden it. The H1 will help with SEO.

Overall

Overall I have enjoyed the process of going back and altering and improving my site, there will be another blog on more improvements I am sure! If you have any improvements or tips for me, please contact me as I am grateful for any feedback.
